Date Accessed: 1/18/12

Brand The only words that really come to mind are colorful, for the bright colors of the socks, and funky because of the positioning of the person's legs - it makes it look like she needs to go to the restroom. That, or the person is extremely shy. Either way, in my opinion, it's not clear or great branding by any means. The page is simple and organized, but that may be attributed to either the fact that the designer likes simplicity and organization, or the fact that it's just a really boring layout.
Functionality I don't really like this site's functionality too much, becuase there really isn't much. For a person who's been in the trade for seven years (at the time of making this portfolio/page), I expect a lot more. First of all, the thumbnails of the portfolio pieces are incredibly small . Above each piece is a small caption which says either "web, logo, icon, or char." I had to guess that char meant character too. What's worst is that the captions aforementioned are so small and in such a hard-to-read script that I got really annoyed. When you click on one of the portfolio pieces, a pop-up of that piece comes up for full viewing, not in a separate window. That's not so bad, but I found an error with the first piece on the page. What seems to say "LOADING" is still partially visible behind the fully-loaded image.

As a final critique, there's only twleve images on show at a time, and there's some small left and right arrows (see screenshot of site) to navigate to more portfolio pieces. It's simple, and works, but I'd prefer a system that shows less and larger images per page (perhaps even on another linked page instead of the home portfolio page). On a final note, when the portfolio piece pops up after it's been clicked, the user finds out shortly that the image popping up is merely an image and isn't linked to the actual site. You have to manually copy-paste into a new tab/window to visit that site. Most annoying of all is that you can't right click images or pop-ups after you click on a portfolio piece.
Interactivity This will be short and sweet since there isn't really much to report. There are small arrows at the bottom of the page near the portfolio pieces that allow you to navigate back and forth. There is absolutely no method of filtering, and you're restricted to viewing twelve super small images at a time. From just clicking once or twice (left or right) on the arrows, you get the feeling, "I don't know when this is going to end or how many more portfolio pieces there are," which is annoying. It's rather simple, and there's no animation at all. Sometimes, if the page isn't loading fast enough, you momentarily see a "no picture" sign replacing every portfolio piece, or at least in the space where the piece should be.
Navigation Navigation is pretty easy. The same backgrop is existent for all the subpages of the site. If you click on a portfolio piece, a popup shows up and you can simply close that tab to get back to viewing what you were just looking at - nothing too fancy, no back button required, etc. That's about the only plus. Since the only thing that is changing on the site at all are the portfolio pieces currently being shown before/after you click an arrow key, you don't really have to know where you are. Within the portfolio pieces as a whole, you have to keep mental note of "where you're at" in terms of toward the beginning or the end in order to go back to a piece you looked at before (because there's no filtering mechanism).

Advanced Grid Example KLASIKHZ

Date Accessed: 1/19/12

Brand I have nothing but great things to say about KLASIKHZ. His real name is Iqwak Bhurji, and he is a recent graduate of UCSD (from a few years back). He's pretty much the biggest name in Bolloywood-Bhangra style Indian music in the Nation, and you can hear his "Klasikhz Klasikhz" audio tag in all of his mixes. It's extremely uniform - always the same audio tag - and the same goes for his logo (KLASIKHZ; it's been the same since day one). I fortunately can say that his brandname, KLASIKHZ, is a perfect embodiment of himself as a person: words like bold, edgy, and classy come to mind when you see his logo and the pictures he posts on his site. The name Klasikhz itself is a clever pun. He uses a ton of "classics" for his mixes and he is Sikh by religion, hence the name Klasikhz. That in itself sort of shows how proud he is in his roots, and in combination with the edgy graphics and pictures on his home page, portfolio page, and the individual track artwork featured in his albums (designed by his little brother), lends website visitors a pretty good idea of what this guy is about. At the bottom of the page, there is a music player which showcases a song of his upon arrival to the site. Within the first few seconds, you hear the famous "klasikhz Klasikhz" audio tag and you immediately know it's his song. As mentioned before, his branding is extremely simplified (logo and audio tag), and you're not going to forget it or him.
Functionality I really don't have any complaints here. To return from any page to the home page, you just click on the huge and hard to miss KLASIKHZ logo at the top of the page. The "About" page is simple - it includes a picture of himself and a bio, and the same for another guy who works with him. The Discography tab sends you to a page with his released material/mixtapes/albums. Upon clicking any link, you get sent to a page which gives you the option to play any individual track, or you can click a link that sends you to a page where you can download the entire album all at once. I was trying to link the page that shows the individual tracks, their album artwork, and other related information because it's really well made and looks awesome, but I just realized that navigating anywhere on the page doesn't change the URL in the address box of the browser. That seems to be a problem, especially if I want to be able to share a particular part of the site with a friend. Also, when clicking the "download album" button, an error comes up if you didn't enter your name and email address. That, for some, may also be a problem. On his portfolio page, you can see all the Universities/Teams he's made mixes for in the past. Upon clicking any team, you're sent to a page which includes a blurb from the captain of that team, a blurb from Klasikhz about the experience he had working on that mix witht that team, a sidebox for easy downloading of the mix(es), the location of the team, a picture of the team, and featured video of the team. Clicking any of the links fades the background and pulls up a new frame on top of the existing page. It makes for fast viewing, and returning to the page you were at earlier is as simple as clicking the "x" on the new media frame. His Downloads page has a nice table for "recently added" and "most popular," as well as a link to "all downloads." His contact page is pretty nice, and it has a link back to his services page that goes directly to his "Booking Request" page. Over all, I can easily find my way around and everything works pretty well.
Interactivity The main page has a banner/slider of some sort, which on a timed interval rotates through a few images. At the bottom left of the page, there's a twitter feed that gets updated whenever someone posts to/about him or his page. At the bottom right of the page is a Facebook feed that shows which of the viewer's friends has "liked" KLASIKHZ. It also provides a link to his facebook page. His new album "For the Record" was recently released and there was a live countdown counter (days, hours, minutes, seconds) until the release of the album. The SCM Music Player is imbedded at the bottom of the page and plays one of his songs when you arrive on the page. You have full control over what song you want to play (which can be accessed by clicking on the "playlist" icon at the bottom right of the music bar, and you also have control over the volume, etc. Finally, the contact page has fill-in sections for Name, E-Mail, Subject, etc. Before you can submit, there's an img captcha mechanism that's been put into place. As for any filtering, the only type of filtering you can do is "by team" on the portfolio page, which is sort of built in to the way people search for mixes. On the downloads page, as mentioned above, there's a "most popular" and "recently added" section, as well as a "all downloads" link. It would be awesome if you could search mixes for specific artists, but that's a rather unrealistic amount of work for KLASIKHZ to go through considering there are tons of artists featured in any given mix. In addition, people don't usually search for remixes like that anyway.
Navigation I think I covered a lot of the actual navigating stuff in my Functionality section above, but to add to that stuff, it's pretty simple to see where on the site you're at. The home page is pretty distinct and easy to remember, so you know when you're on that. At the top of every subpage, there's a header (which should be a bit bigger or bolder) showing which page you're on. Slightly above that header on every page is a small Nav bar which has a link to Home. Interestingly enough, if you click on another subpage link on one of the main subpages, the option to go back one page to the main subpage disappears. All that's left is the sub-subpage link (the page you're currently on) and the link to the homepage.


Only problem I see in navigation is that some sub-pages, ie the Home>Discography>"Any Subsequent Subpage" has a different navigation bar up on the top. It's not so much a problem in Interactivity as it is a problem in consistency.

Featured Photo Example INFINITI

Date Accessed: 1/19/12

Brand Infiniti's logo is at the top left of the page is kind of small at first look, so I definitely don't get that "powerful" feeling I would expect to get from a car company logo. It does look pretty classy however, as well as professional. The logo is metallic, sharp, and crisp. It also matches the color of the script, and coincidentally, the color of the car on slide one of the large feature- photo banner. That uniformity tells me that the car line is probably very unique and has a special design that is pervasive througout all the different types of cars INFINITI makes.
Functionality Functionality of this site is pretty awesome compared to a lot of stuff I've previously seen on other car sites. First of all, the featured photo area has four multimedia slides, one of them being a video. It's super clear to see which slide you're on, and it even shows you how much time is left on that slide. You can even select which slide to view.

All linkable material gets highlighted when you hover over it, which is really nice. There are also two perfectly-timed drowdown menus that you can view stuff from: "What's Inside" and "Infiniti Models." The "What's Inside" dropdown is pretty cool because it links you to more media, pics, videos, etc.

The "Infiniti Models" dropdown is even cooler because when you scroll over any model car, a new description and picture for that model shows up.

Pretty sweet! Also, when you mouse off of the dropdown menus, they go back up in a very timely manner that's not too fast or too slow.
Interactivity The Interactivity of this site is pretty awesome too. It sort of melds together with the Functionality. With regards to the drop down menus, all the links in the "What's Inside" dropdown take you to another "version" of the site entirely dedicated to that specific topic. The links all are highlighted when you hover over them, which makes browsing really easy. The "Infiniti Models" dropdown allows you to scroll over different cars just by using your mouse and doesn't require you to "scroll" with a mouse or to drag a scrollbar, which is kind of neat. Just like with the "What's Inside" links, you're redirected upon clicking any car model to its own home site. That just goes to show that the original home page has been optimized for showcasing all that Infiniti has to offer without plaguing its viewers with excess information and selling points. As mentioned above, you can pause the banner videos or change the multimedia being shown at any time, which is also kind of nice.
Navigation There isn't much to say about the navigation on this page, because it's super simple. You can mess with the banner, or you can click on any of the links and they will take you to the specific product or topic's home page. For the dropdown menus, you're still technically on the home page since it's just a dropdown, so you're back to the page as soon as you mouse off the dropdown tab. When you click on the links that send you to different web pages, there's no real need to navigate back to the original page as it is meant to be a starting point for your INFINITI shopping/learning experience. However, you can always "back" click all the way to it if you'd like. The Infiniti logo at the top actually redirects you to the global INFINITI page, where you have even more option. That is the only flaw, becuase the only easy way to get to this site is by googling "Infiniti" and clicking the first link.

Fixed Sidebar Example Tri-Win Mailing Services

Date Accessed: 1/19/12

Brand Tri-Win mailing services has a little 3-D traingular prism sort of logo, which is a dull red color. The logo looks old to me because it's fairly simple and a little more pixelated than the types of logo's you normally see today. Seems like it's an older, lasting company (lo and behold, this is at the bottom of the site: "Tri-Win is committed to pursuing sustainability when possible. "). The postcard that borders the website makes me think of the word patriotic because of the red, white, and blue alternating colors.
Functionality The Functionality of this website is pretty simple and straightforward. The side-bar provides quick links to everything you'd need to reach on the website. Although a little redundant because it's located twice on the same page, a quick blurb and link to each service provided is at the bottom right hand corner of the page. Half of the links light up, and the rest don't (ie sidebar links don't, but the "request a quote" and "highlighted words" do). The links don't have any text that pops up when you hover over, but that's about the only thing. It's not really even a problem. It is nice, however, that the serivces on the bottom right hand of the page have a little description. It is a mailing service company after all, so they made sure that important piece of information was made abailable for visitors to the site.
Interactivity There isn't much interaction going on while you're on the site. There is no sorting apparent, nor is it really needed. The only thing you need to know is the difference between each of the services, and that's clearly taken care of already. The only other interactivity is when you hover over some links, and they light up.
